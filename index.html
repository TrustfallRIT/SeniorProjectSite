Project Synopsis

The purpose of the project is to automate employer and student interactions during career fairs. This includes signing up for and signing into interviews. Employers will be able to create as many schedules as they need depending on how many interviewers they decide to bring to the interview day. After deciding which students they will interview, employers will be able to schedule the students on any of their schedules as long as the student is available during the chosen interview block. Each student will also have their own schedule which will let them view and check into interviews. When a student checks into an interview, the interviewer gets notified via a change in the status of the interview object on their schedule. Development Methodology

We will be developing using a kanban project management methodology. We believe that with our frequent meetings and structured progress assigned by RIT, we will be able to stay on pace to complete the project without using structured two week sprints, however we still want to use an agile methodology.

Planned Activities and Artifacts

As a team we plan on meeting twice a week in person. Once on Mondays with just our group members, and on Wednesdays with the sponsor and coach. Additionally, we have a discord as a quick online space to meet.

Artifacts will be assigned based on team roles, and members are free to commit code to their branches on their own time. However, before merging or committing actions on the senior project page, artifacts will be briefly reviewed by the team, or the project manager if on short notice.

Roles

Tickets on our Jira board will be associated with roles within our group. User stories associated with the front page design would be given to front-end developer roles, for example.

Standards and Quality Practices

We will have testing roles, and those developers will be expected to complete thorough unit testing.
